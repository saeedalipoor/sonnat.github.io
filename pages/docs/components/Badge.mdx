<h2>
  <span>Filled Badges</span>
  <AnchorButton anchorId="filled-badges" />
</h2>

<p>
  Filled badges show text or number, using primary and secondary colors.
  <br />
  You can use them as a standalone badge or as a relative badge applied to its
  children.
</p>

<DemoBox
  code={[
    '<Badge textContent="NEW" />',
    '<Badge textContent="NEW" color="secondary" />',
    `<Badge textContent="5">

    leadingIcon={<EmailO />}
    variant="inlined"
  />
</Badge>`,
    `<Badge textContent="5" color="secondary">
  <Button
    aria-label="Demo notification button"
    leadingIcon={<EmailO />}
    variant="inlined"
  />
</Badge>`
  ].join("\n")}
>
  <Badge textContent="NEW" className="demoSubject" />
  <Badge textContent="NEW" className="demoSubject" color="secondary" />
  <Badge textContent="5" className="demoSubject">
    <Button
      aria-label="Demo notification button"
      leadingIcon={<EmailO />}
      variant="inlined"
    />
  </Badge>
  <Badge textContent="5" className="demoSubject" color="secondary">
    <Button
      aria-label="Demo notification button"
      leadingIcon={<EmailO />}
      variant="inlined"
    />
  </Badge>
</DemoBox>

<h2>
  <span>Dotted Badges</span>
  <AnchorButton anchorId="dotted-badges" />
</h2>

<p>
  Dotted badge is a small dot indicates that something has changed without
  giving a count.
  <br />
  Just like the filled badges, you can have dotted badges as standalone or
  relative to its child.
</p>

<DemoBox
  code={[
    '<Badge variant="dotted" />',
    '<Badge color="secondary" variant="dotted" />',
    `<Badge variant="dotted">
  <Text variant="caption" rootNode="p">
    This is a typography!
  </Text>
</Badge>`,
    `<Badge variant="dotted" color="secondary">
  <Button
    aria-label="Demo download button"
    leadingIcon={<Download />}
    variant="inlined"
  />
</Badge>`
  ].join("\n")}
>
  <Badge className="demoSubject" variant="dotted" />
  <Badge className="demoSubject" color="secondary" variant="dotted" />
  <div className="demoSubject">
    <Badge variant="dotted">
      <Text variant="caption" rootNode="p">
        This is a typography!
      </Text>
    </Badge>
  </div>
  <Badge className="demoSubject" color="secondary" variant="dotted">
    <Button
      aria-label="Demo download button"
      leadingIcon={<Download />}
      variant="inlined"
    />
  </Badge>
</DemoBox>

<p>
  To have the larger or smaller dotted badges, use the{" "}
  <inlineCode>dotSize</inlineCode> property.
</p>

<DemoBox
  code={[
    '<Badge dotSize="small" variant="dotted" />',
    '<Badge dotSize="medium" variant="dotted" />',
    '<Badge dotSize="large" variant="dotted" />'
  ].join("\n")}
>
  <Badge className="demoSubject" dotSize="small" variant="dotted" />
  <Badge className="demoSubject" dotSize="medium" variant="dotted" />
  <Badge className="demoSubject" dotSize="large" variant="dotted" />
</DemoBox>

<h2>
  <span>Better Placement</span>
  <AnchorButton anchorId="better-placement" />
</h2>

<p>
  You can use the <inlineCode>childShape</inlineCode> property to place the
  badge way more accurately relative to the corner of the wrapped child(ren).
</p>

<DemoBox
  code={[
    `<Badge variant="dotted" childShape="rectangular">
  // Rectangular shape
  <div></div>
</Badge>`,
    `<Badge variant="dotted" childShape="circular">
  // Circular shape
  <div></div>
</Badge>`
  ].join("\n")}
>
  <div className="demoSubject">
    <Badge variant="dotted" childShape="rectangular">
      <div style={{ width: 32, height: 32, backgroundColor: "#eaeaea" }}></div>
    </Badge>
  </div>
  <div className="demoSubject">
    <Badge variant="dotted" childShape="circular">
      <div
        style={{
          width: 32,
          height: 32,
          borderRadius: "50%",
          backgroundColor: "#eaeaea"
        }}
      ></div>
    </Badge>
  </div>
</DemoBox>
